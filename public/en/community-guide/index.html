Our Rider, Open Rider

From my ride records to our shared riding platform.

Orider did not start from a grand business plan.

It grew from one long-time cyclist and developer fixing the things that felt missing during real rides: records, sensors, routes, and group riding.

It is too early to call it finished. Still, I hope Orider can grow into Our Rider and Open Rider: a small gift that the cycling community can use, question, and improve together.

What it is

Focused while riding, thoughtful after the ride

When you are riding, the screen should not get in the way.

After the ride, the record should open up enough to understand the route, the body, the group, and what could be better next time.

Web

Activity details, fitness trends, routes, segments, groups, and events for a larger screen.

App

Speed, distance, maps, sensors, route guidance, and group location sharing while riding.

Direction

Public repositories, a community hub, developer participation, and governance for a rider-owned platform.

Service

The live service is available at orider.co.kr. Some screens require sign-in and ride data, and some parts are still rough.
Honest status

It is closer to a draft we improve together than a finished product

The screens and features already work, but there is still a lot to improve.

There are bugs, some screens need better explanations, and the app and web experience is not yet as smooth as it should be.

So I want to introduce Orider honestly: why it was made, what it tries to solve, and where it needs help.

It is not enough yet. I would like to improve it together.

Open together

From a tool built by one person to a platform improved together

Orider should not become large by looking like one person's closed service.

It should become better because riders bring their own experiences into it. Public repositories and the community hub are the first doors into that process.

Public repository

Starting with the web pages, the project is being prepared for issues, pull requests, reviews, and CI-based contributions.

Community hub

A place where developer ideas and real rider workflows can be discovered, discussed, and improved.

Developer participation

Personal data APIs and recipes can let riders build dashboards, alerts, reports, and automation from their own records.

Why it matters

I hope the records respect a rider's time

Solo days

Speed, heart rate, power, elevation, and routes become useful material for the next ride.

Group days

Location sharing, member activity, courses, and event flow can help riders move together more safely.

Review days

A ride should not end as numbers alone. It should connect to how the body felt and what comes next.
