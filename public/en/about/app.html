The app should be a screen you can trust while riding

During a ride, one number, one warning, or one map position can matter more than a long explanation.

Orider App is a cycling computer that keeps improving GPS recording, BLE sensors, route guidance, and group location sharing.

Real rides are full of variables, so there are still device-specific bugs and stability work left.

Privacy and trust

Location and fitness records should be handled carefully

A cycling app can reveal home-area starts, repeated routes, body condition, and people you ride with.

That is why Orider spends time on permissions, visibility, location sharing, upload recovery, and logging before simply adding more features.

  • Location: Needed for ride records and routes, but visibility and group sharing should be separate choices.
  • Sensors: Heart rate, cadence, and power from rider-connected devices should be recorded reliably.
  • Recovery: Upload retry and logging should reduce record loss when network or auth fails.
  • Parity: Shared logic and tests should reduce differences between Android and iOS.
  • Unfinished: Sensors, background location, upload, and route guidance still need real-ride validation.
