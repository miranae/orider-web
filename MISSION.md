# Mission

Orider started as a small gift from a long-time cyclist to people who love riding.

It is not meant to become a closed product owned by one person or one company. The goal is to grow it into **Our Rider** and **Open Rider**: a rider platform that the cycling community can inspect, improve, and trust.

## What We Optimize For

- Rider trust over ownership growth.
- Clear ride records over engagement tricks.
- Privacy and consent over convenience.
- Community review over closed decisions.
- Small, useful improvements over grand promises.

## Why Community First

Ride records, sensors, routes, training, and group rides are not one person's problem. Every rider has different equipment, roads, goals, and safety concerns. A closed answer from one maintainer will become narrow quickly.

Orider should therefore stay open enough for riders and developers to question how it works, improve the experience, and build useful personal tools around their own data.

## Public Commitment

Orider may need funding, infrastructure support, and paid operational work to stay reliable. That should not turn the community-built core into a closed private asset.

The project will use open licensing, public governance, and contributor protections to make closed privatization difficult and to keep the core platform accountable to riders.
